# Focus Pruning Audit

Research baseline: DBZ-style action RPGs put combat, ki attacks, transformations, training, exploration, and simple quests at the center. Open-world quest research also points toward objectives that arise from the core mechanics instead of unrelated side systems.

## Kept

- Real-time top-down combat with strikes, blasts, guard, dodge, enemy pressure, bosses, XP, and money.
- Training through mentors, sparring, Aura Forge, form mastery, and technique unlocks.
- 2D open-world traversal with region exits, destructibles, loot, route memory, weather, day/night, and flight hazards.
- Quest compass, quest arcs, branch state, and objective rewards.

## Removed From Gameplay

- Figurine Tactics: removed the player button, NPC duel path, figurine loot, type chart, battle modal, rewards, sounds, and smoke requirements.
- Sky Courier: removed the standalone player hotkey, modal minigame, courier cargo loop, rewards, and smoke requirements.

## Replacements

- Figurine objectives became training and sparring objectives.
- Figurine loot became training capsules, spirit tea, or aura charms.
- Toma became a training NPC instead of a collectible-game opponent.
- Flight remains in the world as traversal and hazards instead of a separate courier mode.

## Current Direction

Future builds should bias toward quests, training, combat feel, transformations, bosses, destructible gates, traversal readability, and exploration rewards. New standalone minigames should be rejected unless they directly teach or strengthen one of those loops.
